Introduction

Hello! I’m Gaurav Singh, originally from India and currently evolving my expertise in Machine Learning (ML) at Griffith University, Gold Coast, Australia. My journey combines together a foundation in computer science with an interest for visual arts and a commitment to creative and ethical practice. My work explores ML within human-computer interaction (HCI) and engineering, striving to make data analysis more accessible while considering its societal and ethical dimensions. This focus drives my projects and future ambitions for conscientious technology innovation.

In addition to my computer science background, I've studied Innovation and Experience Design, focusing on creative problem-solving and the societal role of technology. From 2008 to 2015, I worked as an independent designer, developing Mathscapes, a space for creating accessible design tools through mathematics. In 2017, I took a faculty position at Srishti-Manipal, where I taught courses in HCI, mathematics, and programming. My dual roles as designer and researcher have shaped my teaching, offering students unique perspectives on design and technology. In 2022, I left Srishti to pursue further studies, continuing to merge mathematics, computation, and ethics in my work.

Personal background

I was born in Assam, India, and have lived in Delhi, Maharashtra, Rajasthan, Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh, and Karnataka, experiences that have shaped my adaptability and broadened my outlook. My early fascination with science, computers, and mathematics was fueled by interests like A Brief History of Time by Stephen Hawking, Carl Sagan’s Cosmos, and countless popular science media.

I pursued a Bachelor’s in Computer Science and Engineering, which eventually formed my interest in computer vision, algorithms, and image processing. Later, I completed a postgraduate program in Innovation and Experience Design, combining technical skills with creative problem-solving to understand the world around me. This interdisciplinary approach guides my current work at the intersection of mathematics, computer science, and human-computer interaction, with anchors in building accessible, ethical technologies.

Career journey

I began my career as an independent designer, working across a variety of sectors from 2008 to 2015. This experience helped me build a solid foundation in design and technology. In 2017, I joined Srishti Manipal Institute as a faculty member, where I taught courses in programming, mathematics, and human-computer interaction. During my tenure, I facilitated the Experimental Mathematics Lab and led the M.Des. Design Computation program. In 2022, I transitioned to Griffith University in Australia, where I’m now focusing on machine learning applications within engineering, data visualization, and image processing.

Areas of interest

My primary interests lie at the intersection of machine learning, human-computer interaction, and design. I specialize in applying ML techniques to complex data visualization and image processing challenges. Additionally, I explore algorithmic explainability and its role in creating more transparent, user-friendly systems. I’m also intrigued by high-performance visualization tools, optimal graph drawing algorithms, and how they can be applied in various engineering contexts.

Values and philosophies

At the core of my work is a commitment to ethical technology development. I believe in open-access knowledge and designing solutions that prioritize accessibility and user understanding. My approach combines human-centered design (HCD) principles with an emphasis on transparency, fostering technology that empowers users rather than alienating them. I’m also dedicated to balancing technical innovation with societal and ethical considerations, ensuring that my work has a positive, inclusive impact.

Recognition

Throughout my career, I’ve been fortunate to receive several recognitions, including the Gold STEM Professional ICT Award from Griffith Sciences in 2024. I’ve also received research grants from the Adobe Fund for Design in 2021 and 2023, as well as recognition as a Design Champion in the 2017 Topcoder Open. These honors have encouraged me to continue pursuing cutting-edge research and contribute to the broader community.

Teaching, mentorship and leadership

Teaching and mentorship have been integral to my journey. At Srishti Manipal, I led the M.Des. Design Computation program and supervised several award-winning theses in areas like explainable AI and algorithmic literacy. I also established the Experimental Mathematics Lab, fostering interdisciplinary learning by merging design, mathematics, and technology. I am passionate about helping students develop critical thinking skills and guiding them toward innovative solutions in both academic and professional contexts.

Personal interests

Outside of my professional work, I enjoy exploring creative outlets such as art and recreational mathematics. I am passionate about blending these interests into my work, particularly by finding ways to communicate complex ideas through visual means. My curiosity about the natural world, science fiction, and the philosophy of technology also fuels my research and personal projects.

Future directions

Looking ahead, I aim to further explore the convergence of machine learning and human-computer interaction, with a focus on ethical and accessible technology. I plan to expand my research in algorithmic explainability and develop new tools for data visualization and image processing that push the boundaries of transparency and user empowerment. Ultimately, I aspire to contribute to building a more inclusive and thoughtful technological landscape.
