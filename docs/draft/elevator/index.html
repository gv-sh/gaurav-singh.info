Hello! I'm Gaurav, an educator and researcher originally from India, currently developing further expertise in ML at Griffith University in Australia.

My background in computer science, combined with an affinity for visual arts, has led me to specialize in image processing and complex data visualization, aiming to make sophisticated data analysis more accessible while exploring its societal, environmental, and ethical dimensions. With additional background in Innovation and Experience Design, I bring a unique blend of creative experimentation and technological insight, gained from my years as an independent designer and faculty member at the Srishti-Manipal Institute. There, I taught various courses in HCI, mathematics, and programming, leveraging my experience to provide a multifaceted view of design, mathematics, and technology. My current journey at Griffith University continues to be informed by this rich set of experiences, focusing on the meaningful integration of computation, ethics, and creativity in my work.

I have diverse experiences working as a researcher, educator, technologist, and designer, and I am continually seeking collaboration opportunities with like-minded individuals and organizations; the subsequent sections offer a brief overview of the various roles I've undertaken throughout my career.

Educator

In my 7 years at Srishti-Manipal Institute (Srishti) from 2016-2022, I've guided the future of design and technology by teaching 30+ diverse courses and workshops to both undergraduate and postgraduate students. My role has been more than an instructor; I've been a mentor, helped designing curriculums, and several academic events that challenge and inspire students. I've also facilitated Experimental Maths lab, a space for students to explore the intersection of mathematics and design.
