Design Philosophy

Design of my personal website

17.09.24

In the fast-paced digital world, where platforms rise and fall, my personal website stands as a constant — a space of reflection, documentation, and continuity. More than a portfolio or a static information hub, my website serves as a living archive and a biographical book of sorts, chronicling my journey, ideas, and evolution. Unlike social media, which thrives on fleeting updates and algorithmic curation, my website is grounded in a philosophy of permanence and control. Here, I own my narrative. I shape its form, structure, and content to reflect the complexities and nuances of my personal and professional life, capturing not just milestones but the process behind them.

At its core, my website functions as an archive for myself, documenting my thoughts, creative projects, and professional growth. It is a space to showcase accomplishments but a repository for the evolution of my thinking, ideas, and self-expression. This biographical approach allows me to preserve and reflect on how my work has evolved across the years. Having once relied on platforms like Orkut, Yahoo Pages, MySpace, and Facebook, I have experienced firsthand the impermanence of the digital world. With each transition, I lost parts of my digital identity — posts, comments, interactions — fragmented across systems that did not prioritize longevity or user control. The regret of losing content during these transitions deeply influenced my decision to create a personal archive, where nothing is lost unless I choose to remove it. My website is the antidote to that loss, offering a permanent home for my thoughts, where I can curate and preserve every aspect of my creative and intellectual journey.

Social media, while ubiquitous, often feels ephemeral and lacks depth. Content is consumed in fleeting moments, and users have little control over how their work is presented or archived. The very algorithms that dictate visibility often prioritize virality over substance. My website is a deliberate alternative to these platforms. It offers a space for authentic self-expression, free from algorithmic interference and predefined templates. Every element of the design, from the typography to the layout, is a conscious reflection of my personal taste and vision. This autonomy allows me to present my work as a coherent narrative, rather than a fragmented series of updates. In a way, my website is a digital sanctuary — an ongoing project that reflects my creative and intellectual growth, rather than a stream of content designed for immediate consumption.

The need for control over both the design and content of my online presence has been a key driver in shaping the structure of this website. On platforms like Facebook or LinkedIn, I was bound by their design frameworks, which dictated how I presented myself. Here, however, I have full autonomy. I can shape the typography, the layout, and the way information is structured in a manner that feels authentic to my journey. This control extends beyond aesthetics and touches every aspect of my site, ensuring that it is a reflection of my evolving identity. The website itself becomes a design canvas, not just a platform for showcasing work. I experiment with different visual styles, layouts, and narrative structures to express both my professional and creative self. The dynamic nature of this control allows me to craft an online presence that is not just functional but also deeply personal, bridging the gap between professional representation and artistic vision.

One of the central practices behind my website is documentation. For me, documenting is an act of reflection, allowing me to capture thoughts that might otherwise be fleeting. My journal is a important part of this, serving as a repository for personal reflections, technical insights, and creative thoughts. Each entry is a marker of where I am at a specific moment in time, both intellectually and emotionally. Through the act of documenting, I preserve these moments, capturing not only the outcome but the process of how my work has evolved. In one of my posts, “The Evolution of My Personal Website,” I explore how this approach to documentation has shaped the site itself. This living archive allows me to track the progression of ideas, the shifts in my design philosophy, and the ways in which I’ve integrated new technologies into my work. My website is not static; it grows with me, adapting as my thoughts and interests evolve.

The design of my website has been crafted with a strong focus on clarity and flow. The structure is built to serve as a living biographical book, allowing readers to navigate easily between different chapters of my journey. Sections like Education, Work, Projects, Teaching, and Research are designed to give a clear overview of my professional trajectory while maintaining the intimate feel of a personal narrative. The content strikes a balance between technical exploration and personal reflection. From journal entries that capture my thoughts on creativity and exploration to detailed project pages that document my professional challenges and achievements, each section serves to capture the multifaceted nature of my work. This content-rich approach ensures that my site doesn’t merely act as a showcase but as a space for in-depth engagement. Aesthetically, the website embraces minimalism. By using clean layouts, ample whitespace, and carefully chosen typography, I aim to create an environment that allows the content to breathe. I’ve consciously avoided the overstimulation common in modern web design, opting instead for a design that encourages slower, more thoughtful interaction with the material.

Looking ahead, I see my website continuing to evolve as I do. As it stands, it functions as a living archive of my past and present, but in the future, I plan to incorporate more interactive elements. At this moment, I do not envision integrating AI-driven features that dynamically respond to user behavior, creating a more personalized experience for visitors. But some of the features might include interactive project visualizations, dynamic interfaces, or generative design elements that reflect my work in machine learning. Maintainability is also a key focus for the future. I am exploring other mechanisms to ensure the longevity of this archive, free from reliance on any specific platform or service. My commitment to control, documentation, and creative expression will continue to guide the site’s evolution, ensuring that it remains not just a reflection of my past work but a tool for future exploration.

My personal website is much more than a digital portfolio — it is a living, evolving archive of my work, ideas, and creative process. Designed as a biographical book, it captures the full scope of my journey, from technical achievements to artistic exploration. By documenting this evolution, I ensure that my work is preserved and accessible, free from the constraints and impermanence of social media platforms. This essay has explored the design philosophy behind the site, but the story is ongoing. The website will continue to grow, serving as a personal record and creative expression that reflects my evolving identity.
