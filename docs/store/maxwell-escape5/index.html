Year in Review

Ulam Spiral

2009-12-31

2009 was a year full of ups and downs for me. Writing about it fills me with all sorts of feelings. The jump from my first to second year in engineering school was big. The first year was super busy, with classes all day and something always due. It was like a never-ending race. I hardly had a moment to breathe. The second year felt different. It was a bit slower, allowing me to relax more. Even though the courses were still hard, I felt more at ease. Surviving the first year had given me confidence. Engineering is not easy and demands a lot of hard work and interest in the subjects. I learned how challenging engineering can be, especially with tough subjects like engineering math, physics, and hands-on electrical workshops. These classes demanded a strong grasp of hard topics and lots of practice.

Many classmates also found these subjects hard. Together, we got through it by helping each other and often learning from one another rather than from the teachers. One event I'll always remember was the final practical exam in electrical engineering. Walking into that room, I was really nervous. I hadn't attended many practical classes and was scared to take this exam. Luckily, I got an experiment I was good at. Completing it successfully was a big relief and gave me a sense of accomplishment I won't forget.

In addition to school, I also started freelancing this year, which was a life-changer. It let me use the skills I had taught myself in real-world projects. This experience is building important life skills like managing my time, communicating well, and solving problems. Each freelance project brought new challenges and helped me learn and get better. Freelancing also gave me a feeling of independence and some financial freedom, which was a goal of mine. Midway through the year, I had promised myself to start earning money by year's end, and I'm happy I made it happen.

Looking ahead, I can't wait to see what 2010 brings. I feel sure about my skills and am fully committed to reaching my goals. I'm hopeful and excited to keep growing and learning.


UTC+10 Gold Coast, Australia
