Moving from my second to third year in engineering was like shifting from a calm stream to a rushing river. The courses became much more challenging, and there were more projects and deadlines. Each morning, my alarm would signal the start of another demanding day. Classes had a mix of excitement and difficulty. Some topics felt like intricate puzzles waiting to be solved, while others were more like uphill battles. The afternoons became my personal time for hands-on projects. The thrill of converting code into functional applications was deeply satisfying. The library turned into a sanctuary, where I could concentrate and study in peace.

But my days weren't just filled with academics. As evening approached, the atmosphere on campus lightened up. Recreational activities like volleyball and football with friends became the highlight of my daily routine. These games were more than just fun; they were a release from the day’s stress and pressures.

Friendships took center stage this year. My circle remained small, but the bonds strengthened. We not only shared countless study sessions but also embarked on an entrepreneurial venture together. The plan was to create a platform where students could trade textbooks. Getting it off the ground was challenging, yet it offered invaluable lessons in teamwork, persistence, and adaptability.

My weekends, surprisingly, were the most hectic times. They were packed with managing our small business, attending various college events, and taking on freelance assignments. College festivals offered a refreshing change, serving as cultural melting pots and opening up avenues for new experiences.

Freelancing remained a consistent part of my year. Each project offered an opportunity to apply academic knowledge to real-world situations. The experience was enriched by the diversity of clients I got to work with, coming from varied industries and backgrounds.

Perhaps the most significant revelation this year was learning to live in the present. Previously, I’d often find myself consumed by past regrets or future anxieties. However, this year taught me the importance of embracing the present moment. This mental shift has not only increased my focus but has also made me a happier person.
